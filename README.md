# Adventure_works
Adventure Works Cycles Dashboard shows $29.36M sales, $12M profit, 60K customers, and $17.27M costs. Insights reveal Q3 demand peaks, top products like Mountain-200, strong US/Australia markets, and mid-high income buyers. Recommendations: cut costs, grow accessories/clothing, target female customers, expand secondary regions, and build combo offer
Project Overview

Adventure Works Cycles is a multinational bicycle manufacturer headquartered in Bothell, Washington.

The dashboard was created to provide a comprehensive view of company performance across sales, profits, production costs, and customer demographics.

Objective: Transform raw transactional data into actionable insights for strategic decision-making.

Dashboard consolidates multiple data sources into a single interactive view, eliminating manual reporting.

Key Features

Clarity & Usability: Designed for both executives and operational teams.

Interactivity: Enables quick identification of trends and monitoring of KPIs.

Holistic Perspective: Covers financial performance, product analysis, and customer insights.

Key Metrics Overview

Total Sales: $29.36M

Total Profit: $12M

Total Customers: 60K

Total Production Cost: $17.27M

Profit margin ~40%, indicating strong performance but scope for cost optimization.

Sales & Profit Analysis

Monthly trends show production cost fluctuations directly impact profitability.

Quarter-wise sales distribution: Q3 (31%), Q2 (26%), Q1 (24%), Q4 (19%).

Seasonal demand peaks in Q3 linked to launches/promotions.

Identifies inefficiencies in months with high costs but stagnant sales.

Product & Category Insights

Top performers: Mountain-200, Road-150 (>$1M sales each).

Bottom performers: Bike Wash, Racing Socks (minimal contribution).

Category performance: Bikes ($28.32M, 96% of revenue), Accessories ($0.70M sales, $0.44M profit), Clothing (smaller share).

Strategy: Focus on flagship models while investing in underperforming categories.

Customer & Market Analysis

Largest markets: US ($9.39M) and Australia ($9.06M), contributing 60%+ revenue.

Secondary markets: UK, Germany, France, Canada.

Demographics: Strong sales among $70K–$130K income group.

Gender: Male customers dominate; female segment underutilized.

High-value customers: Ashley Henderson, Charles Jackson.

Recommendations

Optimize production costs during low-demand months.

Invest in accessories and clothing for diversification.

Target female customers with tailored campaigns.

Strengthen presence in secondary markets (Canada, France).

Build loyalty programs for top customers.

Conclusion

Dashboard provides actionable intelligence across financial, product, and customer dimensions.

Enables Adventure Works Cycles to enhance efficiency, expand market share, and sustain long-term growth.
